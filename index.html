File 01 — The problem

LA runs on a grid that blinks

This city loses power in three ways, and only one of them sends a warning first. A candle and a junk-drawer flashlight were never the plan — they were the absence of one.

Mode / Scheduled

Shutoffs

Public-safety power shutoffs and maintenance outages come with notice — and still leave the fridge warm, the wifi dead, and the phones counting down.

Mode / Strain

Heat waves

When the whole basin runs AC at once, transformers fail on the hottest days of the year — exactly when losing power is most dangerous.

Mode / No warning

Earthquakes

The one everyone knows is coming and almost no one has powered for. After a major quake, restoration is measured in days, not hours.
